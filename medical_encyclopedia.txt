Influenza (Flu)
Influenza, commonly known as the flu, is a contagious respiratory illness caused by influenza viruses.

Common symptoms of flu include:
- Fever or feeling feverish/chills (not everyone with flu will have a fever)
- Cough
- Sore throat
- Runny or stuffy nose
- Muscle or body aches
- Headaches
- Fatigue (tiredness)
- Some people may have vomiting and diarrhea, though this is more common in children than adults

The flu typically comes on suddenly and symptoms are usually more severe than the common cold.
Most people recover within a few days to less than two weeks. Treatment includes rest, fluids,
and over-the-counter medications. Antiviral drugs may be prescribed in some cases.

Common Cold
The common cold is a viral infection of the upper respiratory tract. It is one of the most
frequent illnesses and is generally harmless, though it can be uncomfortable.

Typical symptoms of the common cold include:
- Runny or stuffy nose
- Sneezing
- Sore throat
- Cough
- Mild headache
- Slight body aches
- Low-grade fever (more common in children)
- General malaise or feeling unwell

Symptoms usually develop gradually and are milder than flu symptoms. Most people recover
within 7-10 days. Treatment focuses on symptom relief through rest, hydration, and
over-the-counter medications.

COVID-19
COVID-19 is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It primarily affects
the respiratory system but can impact other organs.

Common symptoms of COVID-19 include:
- Fever or chills
- Cough (usually dry)
- Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
- Fatigue
- Muscle or body aches
- Headache
- New loss of taste or smell
- Sore throat
- Congestion or runny nose
- Nausea or vomiting
- Diarrhea

Symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure. Some people are asymptomatic while others
develop severe illness requiring hospitalization. Seek immediate medical attention if
experiencing severe symptoms like difficulty breathing or persistent chest pain.

Migraine
A migraine is a neurological condition characterized by intense, debilitating headaches.
Migraines are often accompanied by other symptoms and can significantly impact daily activities.

Symptoms of migraine include:
- Intense throbbing or pulsing pain, usually on one side of the head
- Sensitivity to light (photophobia)
- Sensitivity to sound (phonophobia)
- Nausea and vomiting
- Blurred vision
- Visual disturbances (aura) such as seeing flashing lights or zigzag patterns
- Lightheadedness or dizziness
- Tingling or numbness in the face or extremities

Migraines can last from 4 hours to 3 days. Treatment includes pain relievers, preventive
medications, lifestyle modifications, and avoiding known triggers.

Strep Throat
Strep throat is a bacterial infection caused by group A Streptococcus bacteria. It primarily
affects the throat and tonsils and is highly contagious.

Symptoms of strep throat include:
- Sudden, severe sore throat
- Pain when swallowing
- Red and swollen tonsils, sometimes with white patches or streaks of pus
- Tiny red spots on the roof of the mouth
- Swollen, tender lymph nodes in the neck
- Fever (101Â°F or higher)
- Headache
- Rash (scarlet fever)
- Nausea or vomiting (especially in children)

Unlike viral sore throats, strep throat typically does not cause cough or runny nose.
It requires antibiotic treatment to prevent complications. A throat swab test confirms diagnosis.

Asthma
Asthma is a chronic respiratory condition where airways become inflamed and narrowed,
making breathing difficult. It can range from mild to severe and life-threatening.

Symptoms of asthma include:
- Shortness of breath
- Chest tightness or pain
- Wheezing (a whistling sound when breathing)
- Coughing, especially at night or early morning
- Difficulty breathing or rapid breathing
- Trouble sleeping due to breathing problems
- Fatigue during physical activity

Asthma symptoms can be triggered by allergens, exercise, cold air, stress, or respiratory
infections. Treatment includes avoiding triggers, using inhaled medications (bronchodilators
and corticosteroids), and having an asthma action plan.
